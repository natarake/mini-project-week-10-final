KEY MUSCLES

  • Trapezius

    A pair of large muscles extending over the back of the neck and shoulders and moving the head and shoulder blade.

  • Lateral

    Muscle on the back that stretches to the sides, behind the arm, and is partly covered by the trapezius on the back.

  • Abdominal

    These muscles support the trunk, allow movement and holds organ in place by regulates internal abs pressure.

  • Pectoral

    The pectoral region is located on the anterior chest wall. Contains 4 muscles that exert a force on the upper limb.

  • Quadriceps

    Group of muscles at the front of your thigh used to perform movements like kicking, running, jumping and walking.

  • Calves

    Your calf muscle is very important as it supports you when you stand and enables you to move your foot & lower leg.

  • Hamstrings

    Your hamstring muscles play a vital role in helping you walk, run, bend your knees and extend your legs.

  • Glutes

    These muscles help stabilize the upper body and pelvis, aid in locomotion, and extend the hip.

  • Biceps

    Biceps primarily serves to support and stabilize the brachialis muscle whenever lifting or lowering the forearm

  • Triceps

    Horseshoe shaped muscle on the posterior aspect of the arm. The triceps supports the extension of the elbow joint.
